Order Type	Placed Relative to Market	               What It Does	Typical Use	Key Risk
BUY LIMIT	Below market	Buys at a better (lower) price	        Enter long / take profit on short	No fill if price never drops
SELL LIMIT	Above market	Sells at a better (higher) price	        Enter short / take profit on long	No fill if price never rises
BUY STOP	Above market	Buys when price moves against you	Stop-loss for short	Slippage
SELL STOP	Below market	Sells when price moves against you	Stop-loss for long	Slippage
BUY STOP-LIMIT	        Stop above, Limit ≥ stop	                Stop-loss short with price cap	Controlled exit from short	May not fill
SELL STOP-LIMIT	        Stop below, Limit ≤ stop	               Stop-loss long with price floor	Controlled exit from long	May not fill
Absolute Rules (Read Before Sending)

LIMIT = improve price

STOP = defend position

Short + price going UP = BUY STOP

Long + price going DOWN = SELL STOP

2-Second Sanity Check

“Am I reacting to a worse price?” → STOP
“Am I waiting for a better price?” → LIMIT
